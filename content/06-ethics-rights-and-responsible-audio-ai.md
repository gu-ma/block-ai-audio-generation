# 06 — Ethics, Rights, and Responsible Audio AI

AI audio creation is not only a technical question. It is a question of consent, attribution, transparency, and creative responsibility.

## Key ethical zones in audio generation

## 1) Voice identity and consent

- A voice can function like identity data.
- Always require explicit permission for recognizable voice cloning.
- “Publicly available” does not mean “ethically free to clone.”

## 2) Copyright and style influence

- Generated outputs may still raise copyright or derivative-work questions depending on jurisdiction and usage.
- “Inspired by” prompts should avoid direct imitation of living artists in deceptive ways.

## 3) Disclosure and audience trust

- If synthetic voice/music is used in public contexts, disclosure improves trust.
- In educational and documentary contexts, non-disclosure can be misleading.

## 4) Platform terms and licensing

- Replicate/Hugging Face model cards and licenses differ.
- Always check allowed commercial/non-commercial uses and restrictions.

## Responsible workflow checklist

Before publishing AI-audio work, verify:

1. **Data/voice rights:** consent and provenance are documented
2. **Model rights:** model and output usage aligns with license
3. **Transparency:** synthetic elements are disclosed when needed
4. **Harm check:** no impersonation, manipulation, or deceptive framing
5. **Attribution log:** prompts, tools, versions, and edits are recorded

## Suggested attribution format (class standard)

Include in project notes:

- Tool/platform used
- Model name/version
- Prompt summary
- Human edits (DAW/post-production)
- Rights/consent statement for voices and references

## Practical takeaway

Responsible practice protects people, collaborators, and your future portfolio. Ethical rigor is a creative professionalism skill.
